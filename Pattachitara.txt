Welcome to the captivating world of Pattachitra. Originating from the eastern states of India, particularly Odisha and West Bengal, this ancient art form dates back over a thousand years. The name literally translates to "cloth-picture," born from a sacred tradition of creating scroll paintings to depict religious stories and mythological epics for the historic Jagannath temple.

Look closely at the rich narrative unfolding before you. Pattachitra is celebrated for its intense detail, sharp outlines, and expressive features. The process begins with preparing the canvas, or patta, by bonding layers of cotton cloth with a gum made from tamarind seeds and coating it with chalk powder. This creates a perfectly smooth, leathery surface. Artists then sketch directly onto the fabric without any pencil outlines, displaying phenomenal control and mastery.

The striking brilliance of the artwork comes from pure, natural minerals. The vivid whites are made from crushed conch shells, the deep blacks from lamp soot, and the vibrant reds from local stones. Each color is mixed with natural gum and applied with handmade brushes, giving the painting a glossy, durable finish that allows these scrolls to survive for centuries.

Today, Pattachitra artists continue to preserve this meticulous heritage, blending rigid traditional rules with timeless storytelling. Take a moment to appreciate the incredible precision of the borders and figures, and enjoy the rest of your gallery tour.
